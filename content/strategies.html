    Screen Time

    iPhone / iPad

    Apple’s Screen Time support

    You can use the built-in Screen Time functionality to reduce your device to what you want, in a number of ways:

    • have you been thinking about downgrading to a ‘dumbphone’, but never done so because you need to use specific tools on a smartphone, like WhatsApp? You can turn your iPhone into a ‘dumbphone + WhatsApp’ by going to Settings -> Screen Time -> Content & Privacy Restrictions -> Allowed Apps, then deactivating Safari and other ‘smart’ functionality you don’t want.
    • do you want your iPhone to be a dumbphone only during your working hours? Go to Setting -> Screen Time -> Downtime, and set up a schedule. Then go to Screen Time -> Always Allowed, and select what functionality you want your ‘dumb’ iPhone to have available during those times

      Price: Free

    Full screen mode
    NAApple’s support instructions

    • a basic trick for minimising visual distraction when working on laptop is to place apps you need in full screen mode
  • on Mac, click the green full-screen button to make an app run in full-screen - or click and hold the button to place two app windows side by side
  • in the Safari browser, make a window full-screen, then click ‘View’ and untoggle ‘Always Show Toolbar in Full Screen’ (to make the toolbar appear, bring your cursor to the top of the screen)
  • in the Edge browser, select the ‘Settings and more’ menu, then click the “Full screen” arrows icon

    Go grey scale

    Turn your phone in grey scale mode to avoid visual distraction from shiny icons

    Greyscale

    iPhone / iPad
    Android

    NAAndroid quick settings

    App icons and notifications markers are designed to be eye-catching. Putting your phone in grey scale can tone down their allure. For this to work, you need to be able to toggle it on and off easily, because you will want to turn colour back on for some functionality (like maps)

  • on iPhone, you can set a triple-tap on the Home button to toggle grey scale on and off, by going to: Settings > Accessibility > Accessibility Shortcut > Colour Filters

  • on Android, newer versions (11+) you can add a gray scale toggle to your quick settings by: (1) From the top of your screen, swipe down twice, (2) At the bottom left, tap Edit (3) Touch and hold the grayscale setting symbol, then drag the setting to where you want it. (4) To add a setting, drag it up from “Hold and drag to add tiles.”, (5) To remove a setting, drag it down to “Drag here to remove.”
